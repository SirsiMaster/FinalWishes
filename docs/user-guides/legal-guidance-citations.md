# Legal Guidance Citations

FinalWishes can use an approved legal reference library when you ask Shepherd about probate, advance directives, powers of attorney, estate taxes, or similar legal topics.

When a legal reference is available, Shepherd includes citations with the answer. Each citation points to a specific law, rule, or official source in the FinalWishes corpus.

If Shepherd cannot find a supporting source in the approved corpus, it will say that it cannot answer from the corpus instead of guessing.

Shepherd guidance is informational only. It is not legal advice. For decisions about your estate, a probate filing, a trust, a tax question, or a healthcare directive, review the information with a licensed attorney in your state.

## What To Expect

- Legal answers may include a citations list.
- Citation previews show the source reference and a short excerpt.
- FinalWishes uses official legal sources for the launch corpus.
- Your estate details are not added to the legal corpus.
- If the question is outside the approved corpus, Shepherd may decline to answer.
