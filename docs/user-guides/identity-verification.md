# Identity Verification for Designated Roles

## What It Does
If you've been designated as an **heir**, **executor**, **legal professional**, or **CPA** for someone's estate, FinalWishes requires you to verify your identity before you can access their estate documents. This protects the estate owner and ensures only authorized individuals can view sensitive information.

## What You'll Need to Complete

### Step 1: Enable Two-Factor Authentication
Before anything else, you'll need to set up 2FA on your account. See the [Two-Factor Authentication guide](./two-factor-authentication.md) for step-by-step instructions.

### Step 2: Sign an Identity Attestation
This is a digital declaration confirming you are who you say you are. Here's what happens:

1. **Read the Declaration**: You'll see a legal statement that says you are the person designated for this role. Read it carefully.

2. **Draw Your Signature**: Using your mouse or finger (on mobile), sign your name in the signature box.

3. **Check the Box**: Confirm that you understand this is a legally binding document.

4. **Submit**: Click "Sign & Submit Attestation."

## What the Attestation Says
The attestation states:
> "I, [Your Name], attest under penalty of perjury that I am the individual designated as [Your Role] for the [Estate Name] estate."

It also confirms that:
- False information may result in legal consequences
- Your access depends on the accuracy of this statement
- The attestation is stored permanently
- The estate owner can revoke your access at any time

## After Verification
Once both steps are complete, you'll have full access to the estate's:
- Dashboard and overview
- Document vault
- Asset inventory
- Beneficiary information
- Obituary/memorial records
- Notification center

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Why do I need to do this?**
A: Estate documents contain extremely sensitive personal, financial, and legal information. Identity verification ensures only the right people can access them.

**Q: Can the estate owner see that I've verified?**
A: Yes. They receive a notification when you complete your identity verification.

**Q: Can the estate owner remove my access?**
A: Yes. The estate owner (principal) can revoke your access at any time.

**Q: I was already verified for one estate. Do I need to verify again for another?**
A: Yes. Each estate requires a separate attestation, because each one is a separate legal relationship.
