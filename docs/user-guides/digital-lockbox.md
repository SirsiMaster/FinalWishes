# Digital Lockbox

> Store your most sensitive credentials -- bank logins, email passwords, crypto wallets -- in an encrypted vault that only you (and your executor, when the time comes) can access.

## What You'll Need
- An active FinalWishes account with at least one estate

## Step by Step

### What to Store
The Digital Lockbox is designed for credentials your family would need to manage your affairs:
- **Banking & Financial** -- online banking logins, brokerage accounts, crypto wallet keys
- **Email & Communication** -- email passwords, messaging app PINs
- **Social Media** -- Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn (for memorialization or closure)
- **Utilities & Services** -- streaming accounts, insurance portals, utility logins
- **Other** -- security system codes, safe combinations, storage unit access

### Adding a Credential
1. Navigate to your estate dashboard and click **Digital Lockbox** in the sidebar
2. Click **Add Credential**
3. Select a category (Financial, Email, Social, Utility, or Other)
4. Enter the service name (e.g., "Bank of America"), username, and password
5. Add **transition instructions** -- tell your executor what to do with this account (e.g., "Close account and transfer balance to Sarah" or "Memorialize this profile")
6. Click **Save**

### How Encryption Protects Your Credentials
Every username and password you enter is encrypted before it's stored. Think of it like a lockbox inside a lockbox -- your data is scrambled using military-grade encryption (AES-256) and can only be unscrambled with a key that's stored separately in Google Cloud's security system. No one at FinalWishes can read your credentials.

### Viewing Stored Credentials
1. Click on any entry to reveal the stored credential
2. The password is displayed for **30 seconds**, then automatically cleared from your screen
3. This auto-clear protects you if you step away from your computer

### Transition Instructions for Your Heirs
Each credential entry includes a space for instructions. Write clear, specific directions:
- "Close this account and transfer remaining funds to checking account #XXXX"
- "Contact customer service at 1-800-XXX-XXXX to close"
- "This account should be memorialized, not deleted"

## Tips
- Update your lockbox whenever you change a password.
- Be specific in your transition instructions -- your executor may not be tech-savvy.

## FAQ
**Q: Can my executor see my passwords right now?**
A: No. Lockbox credentials are only accessible to your executor after your passing is confirmed.

**Q: What if I forget to update a password here?**
A: Review your lockbox periodically. The Shepherd AI will remind you if it's been a while since your last update.
