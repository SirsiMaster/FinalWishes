# Video Memorials

> Preserve your voice, your face, and your stories in video -- the most personal way to leave a lasting message for your family.

## What You'll Need
- An active FinalWishes account with at least one estate
- A YouTube account (for video hosting)

## Step by Step

### Uploading a Video via YouTube
FinalWishes uses YouTube to securely store and stream your videos. Here's how:
1. Navigate to your estate dashboard and click **Memoirs** in the sidebar
2. Click **Upload Video**
3. Select a video file from your computer
4. Add a title and description (e.g., "Message to My Children" or "The Story of How We Met")
5. The video is uploaded to YouTube as **unlisted** -- meaning only people with the direct link can view it
6. Click **Save**

### Adding an Existing YouTube Link
If you've already uploaded a video to YouTube:
1. Click **Add Video Link** instead of uploading
2. Paste the YouTube URL
3. Add a title and description
4. Click **Save**

### Uploading Photos
1. From the Memoirs page, click **Upload Photo**
2. Select one or more images from your computer
3. Add a caption or story for each photo -- this context is what makes a photo a memory
4. Click **Save**

### Privacy Controls
- **Private Archive** -- Only you can see this video or photo
- **Share with Heirs** -- Choose specific heirs who can view this memory after your estate enters settlement
- You can change the visibility setting at any time before your estate is in settlement

## Tips
- You don't need professional equipment. A smartphone video with honest words is more meaningful than a polished production.
- Record short videos for specific people. A 2-minute message to each grandchild is worth more than a 30-minute general recording.
- Add captions to photos while the memories are fresh. Who are these people? Where was this? What happened that day?

## FAQ
**Q: Can my heirs see my videos while I'm alive?**
A: Only if you set the visibility to "Share with Heirs." Private Archive videos remain hidden until your estate enters settlement.

**Q: What happens if I delete the video from YouTube?**
A: The memorial entry in FinalWishes will remain, but the video will no longer be playable. Keep your YouTube videos intact.
