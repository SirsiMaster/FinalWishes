# Heirloom Registry

> Catalog your most treasured physical belongings -- the jewelry, artwork, furniture, and keepsakes that carry your family's story -- and designate who should receive each one.

## What You'll Need
- An active FinalWishes account with at least one estate

## Step by Step

### Adding a Physical Item
1. Navigate to your estate dashboard and click **Assets** in the sidebar
2. Click **Add Asset** and select the **Personal Property** category
3. Enter a name for the item (e.g., "Grandmother's Diamond Ring" or "Oil Painting from 1952")
4. Add a description, including where the item is physically located
5. Upload a photo so your family can identify it
6. Enter an estimated value (for estate planning purposes)
7. Click **Save**

### Assigning a Designated Heir
1. Open any heirloom entry from your asset list
2. In the **Designated Heir** field, select the person who should receive this item
3. Click **Save Changes**
4. The heir will not be notified until the estate enters settlement

### Recording Provenance
Provenance is the story behind an item -- where it came from, who owned it before you, and why it matters. For each heirloom:
1. Use the **Description** field to write the item's history
2. Include details like: who gave it to you, what year, any special occasions tied to it
3. This context turns a physical object into a meaningful family legacy

## Tips
- Take clear, well-lit photos of each item. Include close-ups of any distinguishing marks or engravings.
- Note the physical location of each item (e.g., "Top shelf of the bedroom closet" or "Safety deposit box at First National Bank").
- Items with sentimental value are just as important as items with monetary value.

## FAQ
**Q: Can I assign the same item to two people?**
A: Each item is designated to one heir. If you'd like to note a shared arrangement, include the details in the description field.

**Q: What if I sell or give away an item?**
A: Update or remove the entry from your asset list to keep your estate plan current.
