# Estate Settlement (Probate)

> A guided walkthrough of the Illinois probate process -- deadlines, court forms, executor activation, and death certificate review -- all in one place.

## What You'll Need
- An active FinalWishes account with an estate in settlement
- Executor or administrator role on the estate
- A copy of the death certificate (PDF or image)

## Overview

When an estate owner passes away, FinalWishes transitions the estate into settlement mode. The Estate Settlement page guides executors through the Illinois probate process step by step, tracking deadlines and generating the forms you'll need for Cook County court.

## Step by Step

### 1. Death Certificate Review

After a death is reported, FinalWishes uses AI to analyze the uploaded death certificate and extract key facts:
- Full legal name of the deceased
- Date of death
- Place of death
- Cause of death

You'll be asked to **review and confirm** these facts before proceeding. This ensures the data used in court forms is accurate.

### 2. Executor Activation

If you are named as executor in the will:
1. FinalWishes shows your executor status card
2. Click **Confirm Executor Role** to formally activate your authority
3. Once confirmed, you gain full access to settlement tools

### 3. Probate Checklist

The probate checklist is a 17-item guide specific to Illinois law and Cook County procedures. Items include:

- Obtain certified death certificates (5-10 copies recommended)
- Locate and file the original will with Cook County Probate Division
- Petition for Letters of Administration (form CCP0302) or Letters Testamentary (form CCP0315)
- Notify beneficiaries and creditors
- File inventory within 60 days of receiving letters
- Publish notice to creditors (6-month claim period)
- File final accounting and petition for discharge

Each item has a toggle switch -- mark items complete as you work through them. Your progress percentage updates in real time.

### 4. Deadlines

The Settlement page displays all active deadlines with color-coded urgency:
- **Overdue** -- Red, requires immediate attention
- **Due soon** -- Amber, within the next 14 days
- **Upcoming** -- Normal priority

Key Illinois deadlines:
| Deadline | Timeframe |
|----------|-----------|
| File will with court | As soon as practicable |
| Inventory due | 60 days from letters |
| Creditor claims period | 6 months from publication |
| Small estate threshold | $150,000 (vehicles excluded) |

### 5. Court Forms

FinalWishes provides pre-filled form templates for Cook County:
- **CCP0199** -- Cover sheet
- **CCP0315** -- Petition to probate will
- **CCP0302** -- Petition for administration (intestate)
- **CCP0305** -- Affidavit of heirship

Forms are pre-populated with estate data (names, dates, addresses). Download, print, and file with the Cook County Probate Division.

### 6. Next Action Guidance

The "Next Action" card always tells you what to do next based on your current phase:
- If the death certificate hasn't been confirmed: "Review the death certificate"
- If the executor hasn't activated: "Confirm your executor role"
- If there are overdue deadlines: "Address overdue items"
- If checklist items remain: "Continue working through the checklist"

This ensures you are never stuck wondering what comes next.

## Probate Phases

| Phase | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Pre-Filing** | Gathering documents, confirming death, activating executor |
| **Filing** | Submitting petition and will to court |
| **Administration** | Inventorying assets, notifying creditors, managing estate |
| **Distribution** | Distributing assets to beneficiaries per the will or intestacy law |
| **Closing** | Filing final accounting, petition for discharge |

## Tips
- Order at least 5 certified copies of the death certificate -- banks, insurers, and the court all need originals.
- The small estate affidavit ($150,000 threshold, vehicles excluded) can avoid full probate entirely if the estate qualifies.
- Illinois allows independent administration (forms CCP1015/CCP1014) which reduces court oversight and speeds the process.
- Keep all receipts and records -- the final accounting requires a detailed ledger of every transaction.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need a lawyer to use the probate tools?**
A: FinalWishes provides guidance and form preparation, not legal advice. For complex estates or contested wills, we recommend consulting an Illinois probate attorney. The tools are designed to help you stay organized and meet deadlines regardless.

**Q: What if the estate is in a state other than Illinois?**
A: The probate engine currently supports Illinois (Cook County). Support for Maryland and Minnesota is planned. For other states, the general checklist structure applies but specific forms and deadlines will differ.

**Q: Can multiple executors use the settlement tools?**
A: Currently, one executor activates at a time. Multi-executor quorum (2-of-3 approval) is planned for a future release.

**Q: What happens to the probate data if the estate is closed?**
A: All probate records (checklist state, forms downloaded, deadlines tracked) are preserved in the estate's permanent record even after closing.
