# Illinois Estate Planning Guide

This guide covers every document, form, and process available to Illinois residents for estate planning, probate avoidance, and estate settlement. FinalWishes helps you track, complete, and organize all of these.

---

## Advance Directives

Illinois law recognizes four types of advance directives that protect your medical autonomy if you become unable to communicate or make decisions.

### 1. Health Care Power of Attorney

Appoint a trusted person (your "agent") to make medical decisions on your behalf if you become incapacitated.

- **What it covers:** Everyday treatment choices, medical record access, and critical end-of-life decisions
- **Who cannot serve:** Your primary doctor or health care provider cannot be your agent
- **Why it matters:** This is the most flexible advance directive because your agent can react to unexpected medical scenarios in real time
- **Form:** Illinois Statutory Short Form Power of Attorney for Health Care
- **Source:** [Illinois Medical Society](https://www.isms.org/resources/patients/personal-decision)

### 2. Living Will

A written declaration that you wish to withhold or withdraw "death-delaying procedures" if you are diagnosed with a terminal condition.

- **What it covers:** Ventilators, CPR, feeding tubes, and other interventions that only prolong the dying process
- **Important limitation:** In Illinois, a Living Will is narrowly tailored to terminal illnesses. It is overridden if you have an active Health Care Power of Attorney agent available to make decisions
- **Form:** Illinois Living Will Declaration
- **Source:** [CaringInfo — Illinois Forms](https://www.caringinfo.org/wp-content/uploads/Illinois.pdf)

### 3. Declaration for Mental Health Treatment Preference

Allows you to dictate choices for future mental health care while you are still of sound mind.

- **What it covers:** Three specific areas: psychotropic medications, electroconvulsive therapy (ECT), and short-term admission to a mental health facility
- **Duration:** Valid for three years from execution (unlike other directives). Must be re-executed to remain active
- **Revocation:** Can be revoked while you are of sound mind
- **Legal basis:** Mental Health Treatment Preference Declaration Act
- **Source:** [Illinois Department on Aging](https://ilaging.illinois.gov/aboutus/legal-adv-directives.html)

### 4. Practitioner Orders for Life-Sustaining Treatment (POLST)

Actionable medical orders signed by both you (or your legal proxy) and your attending physician. Typically for individuals who are seriously ill or near end of life.

- **What it covers:** "Do Not Resuscitate" (DNR) instructions, selective treatment limits, intubation preferences, and feeding tube decisions
- **How it differs:** Unlike other directives, POLST creates immediate medical orders that emergency responders and hospital staff must follow
- **Requires:** Both patient/proxy AND practitioner signatures
- **Form:** IDPH Uniform POLST
- **Source:** [Shirley Ryan AbilityLab — POLST Forms](https://www.sralab.org/lifecenter/resources/listing-advance-directives-forms-health-choices-idhp-and-polst)

### Execution Requirements for All Advance Directives

- **No lawyer needed:** You can complete any of these documents yourself without legal counsel or a notary
- **Witnesses:** Most Illinois directives require at least one witness (18 years or older)
- **Revocation:** You can cancel or alter any directive at any time by destroying the form, writing a cancellation, or verbally stating your intent
- **Forms available:** [Illinois Department of Public Health](https://dph.illinois.gov/topics-services/health-care-regulation/nursing-homes/advance-directives.html)

## Powers of Attorney

### Statutory Short Form Power of Attorney for Property

Appoint an agent to manage your finances, pay bills, and handle property transactions during your incapacity.

- **Scope:** Can be as broad or narrow as you choose
- **Notarization:** Required if involving real estate transactions
- **Successor agents:** You may name backup agents
- **Form:** [Illinois Statutory Short Form POA for Property (PDF)](https://www.illinois.gov/content/dam/soi/en/web/gac/forms/documents/poa-property-july2011.pdf)

### Statutory Short Form Power of Attorney for Healthcare

This is the statutory form version of the Health Care Power of Attorney described above.

- **Form:** Illinois Statutory Short Form Power of Attorney for Health Care
- **Source:** [Governor's Advance Directive Forms](https://gac.illinois.gov/content/dam/soi/en/web/gac/osg/documents/advdir2025.pdf)

## Disposition of Remains

### Appointment of Agent to Control Disposition of Remains

Names an agent who controls funeral, burial, or cremation decisions after your death.

- **Legal basis:** 755 ILCS 65 (Disposition of Remains Act)
- **Requirements:** Signed by you and your agent, notarized
- **Effective:** Upon your death
- **Scope:** All decisions including cremation, burial location, and funeral arrangements are binding
- **Source:** [Illinois Disposition of Remains Act](https://www.ilga.gov/legislation/ilcs/ilcs3.asp?ActID=2737&ChapterID=60)

### Anatomical Gift Document (Organ and Tissue Donation)

Specifies your wishes regarding organ and tissue donation.

- **Options:** Donate specific organs/tissues, donate to a specific recipient or organ bank, or donate your whole body for medical research
- **Registry:** You can register in the First Person Consent registry maintained by the Illinois Secretary of State
- **Important:** Let your healthcare agent know your wishes and registration status

## Probate Avoidance Tools

Illinois offers several ways to transfer assets directly to beneficiaries without going through probate court.

### Transfer on Death Instrument (TODI) — Real Estate

Allows you to name a beneficiary who will receive your Illinois real property when you die, without probate.

- **Requirements:**
  - Signed by the property owner
  - Witnessed by two credible witnesses (neither can be the beneficiary or their spouse)
  - Notarized
  - Recorded with the Recorder of Deeds in the county where the property is located
  - Must be recorded before the owner's death
- **Beneficiaries:** Can be any person, company, trust, or government entity. Multiple beneficiaries allowed
- **Revocation:** Owner can revoke or change at any time while alive
- **After death:** Beneficiary should file a Notice of Death Affidavit and Acceptance within two years
- **Filing fee:** $45-$100 depending on county and page count
- **Legal basis:** 755 ILCS 27 (Real Property Transfer on Death Instrument Act), effective January 1, 2012
- **Forms:** Available from [Illinois Legal Aid Online](https://www.illinoislegalaid.org/legal-information/transfer-death-instrument-or-todi), Cook County Recorder, or an attorney
- **Source:** [Illinois Legal Aid — Creating a TODI](https://www.illinoislegalaid.org/legal-information/creating-transfer-death-instrument-todi)

### Secretary of State Beneficiary Affidavit (VSD 773) — Vehicles

Designate a beneficiary on your vehicle title so it transfers on death without probate.

- **Requirements:**
  - Vehicle must be solely owned (no joint tenancy, no lien, no lease, not a business)
  - Only one beneficiary may be listed on the title
- **Important:** Once the owner dies, the beneficiary designation cannot be changed by a will or any other instrument — only by court order
- **Revocation:** Owner can change or revoke the designation while alive
- **Form:** [VSD 773 — Beneficiary Affidavit (PDF)](https://www.ilsos.gov/content/dam/publications/pdf_publications/vsd773.pdf)

### Secretary of State Beneficiary Claim Form (VSD 774) — After Death

Used by the beneficiary to claim a vehicle after the owner's death.

- **Requirements:** Title (if available), certified death certificate, title fee, appropriate tax form
- **Form:** [VSD 774 — Beneficiary Claim Form (PDF)](https://www.ilsos.gov/content/dam/publications/pdf_publications/vsd774.pdf)

### Payable on Death (POD) — Bank Accounts

Add a "payable-on-death" designation to savings accounts, checking accounts, or certificates of deposit.

- **How it works:** You retain full control of the money during your lifetime. At death, the beneficiary claims the funds directly from the bank with a death certificate
- **No probate required**
- **Legal basis:** 205 ILCS 625 (Illinois Trust and Payable on Death Accounts Act)

### Transfer on Death (TOD) — Securities and Brokerage Accounts

Register stocks, bonds, and brokerage accounts in transfer-on-death form.

- **How it works:** Complete TOD forms with your brokerage. After death, the beneficiary works directly with the brokerage to transfer the account
- **No probate required**

### Beneficiary Designations — Retirement and Insurance

Life insurance policies, 401(k) plans, IRAs, and pensions transfer directly to designated beneficiaries.

- **How it works:** Name beneficiaries through your provider. These designations override your will
- **Important:** Review and update beneficiary designations after major life events (marriage, divorce, birth of children)

## Small Estate Affidavit

When the estate is small enough, you can avoid formal probate entirely.

### Illinois Small Estate Affidavit (Updated 2026)

- **Threshold:** $150,000 (increased from $100,000 effective August 15, 2025)
- **Vehicles excluded:** Motor vehicles do NOT count toward the $150,000 limit
- **Waiting period:** Must wait at least 30 days after date of death
- **Real estate excluded:** Cannot transfer real property via small estate affidavit
- **Requirements:**
  - No probate case is open or anticipated
  - Affiant's full name, mailing address, and residential address
  - Decedent's name, date of death, and last address
  - Asset inventory with fair market values
  - Certified death certificate attached
  - Out-of-state residents must name an Illinois agent for service of process
- **Obligation:** By signing, you commit to pay the decedent's debts before distributing any money
- **Forms:** [Illinois Legal Aid — Small Estate Affidavit Easy Form](https://www.illinoislegalaid.org/legal-information/using-small-estate-affidavit) or [Secretary of State Form 3606](https://ilsrs.illinois.gov/content/dam/soi/en/web/srs/sers/resources/documents/form-downloads/3606.pdf)

## Cook County Probate Court Forms

When formal probate IS required, these are the forms used in Cook County (Chicago). Other Illinois counties use similar forms available from the Illinois Supreme Court.

### General Forms

| Form | Number | Purpose |
|------|--------|---------|
| Probate Division Cover Sheet | CCP0199 | Required cover page for all filings |
| Notice of Motion | CCG0003 | Notify parties of court motions |
| Oath and Bond of Representative (Surety) | CCP0312 | Representative's oath with surety bond |
| Oath and Bond of Representative (No Surety) | CCP0313 | Representative's oath without surety bond |

### Decedent Estate Forms

| Form | Number | Purpose |
|------|--------|---------|
| Petition for Probate of Will and Letters Testamentary | CCP0315 | Open probate when a will exists |
| Petition for Letters of Administration | CCP0302 | Open probate when no will exists (intestate) |
| Petition for Letters of Administration to Collect | CCP0301 | Limited authority to collect and preserve assets |
| Petition for Probate + Letters with Will Annexed | CCP0316 | When will names no executor or executor cannot serve |
| Order Declaring Heirship | CCP0305 | Court order identifying legal heirs |
| Order Admitting Will + Appointing Representative | CCP0319 | Court approval of will and representative |
| Order Appointing Representative (Intestate) | CCP0314 | Court appointment when no will exists |
| Waiver of Notice | CCP0303 | Heirs waive formal notice of proceedings |
| Copy of Will | CCP0317 | Certified copy of the will for court |
| Order Converting to Independent Administration | CCP1015 | Switch from supervised to independent administration |
| Order Converting to Independent Admin for Closing | CCP1014 | Convert to independent admin specifically to close |
| Affidavit of Heirship | (Sample forms) | Sworn statement identifying heirs |

### Key Probate Deadlines (Illinois)

| Deadline | Timeframe | Description |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Inventory | 60 days | File inventory of all estate assets after appointment |
| Creditor Notice Publication | Promptly | Publish notice in newspaper after appointment |
| Creditor Claims Period | 6 months | Creditors must file claims within 6 months of publication |
| Final Accounting | Before closing | Detailed accounting of all estate transactions |
| Receipt and Release | Before closing | Beneficiaries sign acknowledgment of distributions |

### Source

All Cook County probate forms are available at:
- [Circuit Court of Cook County — Probate Forms](https://www.cookcountycourtil.gov/division/probate-division/court-forms-probate-division)
- [Clerk of the Circuit Court Forms](https://services.cookcountyclerkofcourt.org/forms/)

## Trust Documents

FinalWishes provides guidance on trust planning but does not generate trust documents. Consult an attorney for:

- **Revocable Living Trust** — manage property during lifetime, avoid probate at death, can be amended or revoked
- **Irrevocable Trust** — cannot be changed after creation, may exclude assets from taxable estate

## Illinois Tax Thresholds

| Tax | Threshold | Notes |
|-----|-----------|-------|
| Illinois Estate Tax | $4 million | Estates above this owe state estate tax |
| Federal Estate Tax | ~$13.6 million (2026) | Subject to federal legislative changes |
