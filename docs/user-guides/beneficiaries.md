# Family & Heirs

> Designate the people who matter most -- the executors who will manage your estate and the heirs who will inherit your legacy.

## What You'll Need
- An active FinalWishes account with at least one estate
- Email addresses for the people you want to add

## Step by Step

### Adding Family Members
1. Navigate to your estate dashboard and click **Beneficiaries** in the sidebar
2. Click **Add Executor** or **Add Heir**
3. Enter the person's name and email address
4. Choose their role (see below)
5. Click **Send Invitation**

### Roles Explained
- **Primary Executor** -- The person responsible for managing your estate after your passing. They gain full access after a 72-hour confirmation period.
- **Backup Executor** -- Steps in if your primary executor is unable to serve.
- **Heir** -- A person designated to receive specific assets, messages, or time capsules. They can only view what you've shared with them.
- **Trustee** -- A person managing assets on behalf of a minor heir.

### How Invitations Work
1. When you add someone, they receive an email invitation from FinalWishes
2. They click the link to create their own account (or log in if they already have one)
3. Once they accept, their status changes from **Pending** to **Accepted**
4. If they decline, you'll be notified so you can designate someone else

### What Beneficiaries Can See
- **While you are alive:** Executors and heirs can see that they've been designated, but they cannot access your documents, assets, or credentials.
- **After confirmation of passing:** Executors gain full access to manage the estate. Heirs receive only the specific items, messages, and time capsules you've directed to them.

## Tips
- Designate at least two executors in case one is unavailable.
- If an heir is a minor, assign a guardian or trustee to manage their inheritance.

## FAQ
**Q: Can I remove someone after I've added them?**
A: Yes. Click the remove icon next to their name. They will lose access immediately.

**Q: Will my heirs know what I've left them before I pass?**
A: No. Heirs cannot see asset designations, directives, or time capsules until your estate enters settlement.
