# Getting Started with FinalWishes

> FinalWishes is your secure estate planning platform -- a place to organize your legacy while you're alive so your family never has to scramble.

## What You'll Need
- A valid email address
- A smartphone with an authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Authy, or 1Password)

## Step by Step

### Creating Your Account
1. Visit **FinalWishes** and click **Get Started**
2. Enter your name, email address, and a strong password
3. Check your inbox for a verification email and click the confirmation link

### Setting Up Multi-Factor Authentication
Multi-factor authentication (MFA) adds a second layer of protection -- essential when your account holds sensitive estate information.

1. After verifying your email, you'll be prompted to set up MFA
2. Open your authenticator app and scan the QR code displayed on screen
3. Enter the 6-digit code from your app to confirm
4. MFA is now active -- you'll enter a code each time you log in

### Creating Your First Estate
1. From the home screen, click **Create Estate**
2. Give your estate a name (e.g., "The Collymore Family Estate")
3. You are automatically assigned as the **Principal** -- the owner of this estate

### Navigating the Dashboard
Your dashboard is your command center. You'll see:
- **Completion Score** -- how much of your legacy is organized (powered by our AI assistant, The Shepherd)
- **Quick Actions** -- shortcuts to upload documents, add assets, or record a memory
- **Recent Activity** -- a live feed of changes to your estate
- **Notification Bell** -- alerts for pending invitations and important updates

## Tips
- Start with what you have. Even uploading one document gets you started.
- The Shepherd AI will suggest what to do next based on what you've already added.

## FAQ
**Q: Can I manage more than one estate?**
A: Yes. You can be the Principal on your own estate and an Executor or Heir on someone else's.

**Q: Is MFA required?**
A: MFA is optional for estate owners but strongly recommended. It is required for Executors, Heirs, and legal professionals.
