# Final Directives

> Write your final wishes -- from funeral preferences to life lessons -- in your own words, on your own terms.

## What You'll Need
- An active FinalWishes account with at least one estate

## Step by Step

### Types of Directives
FinalWishes supports four types of final directives:
- **Ethical Will** -- Share your values, life lessons, and hopes for future generations. This is not a legal document; it's a personal letter to the people you love.
- **Funeral Preferences** -- Burial or cremation, location, music, readings, and who should attend.
- **Final Message** -- A personal letter to a specific person or your family as a whole.
- **Care Instructions** -- Guidance for pets, ongoing responsibilities, or anything that needs attention after your passing.

### Creating a Directive
1. Navigate to your estate dashboard and click **Directives** in the sidebar
2. Click **New Directive**
3. Choose the type of directive you want to create
4. Give it a title (e.g., "Letter to My Grandchildren" or "Funeral Wishes")
5. Write your content using the rich text editor -- you can format text, add headings, and create lists
6. Choose who should receive this directive (one or more heirs)
7. Click **Save as Draft**

### Finalizing a Directive
When you're satisfied with a directive, click **Finalize**. This locks the document:
- A finalized directive cannot be edited (to preserve its legal and personal integrity)
- You'll be asked to review and digitally sign it through our secure signing process
- A 72-hour window is provided after finalizing, during which you can unseal it if you change your mind

### Exporting to PDF
Click the **Export PDF** button on any directive to download a beautifully formatted, printable version. This is useful for sharing with your attorney or keeping a physical copy.

## Tips
- Start with what feels easiest. Many people begin with funeral preferences because the decisions are concrete.
- There's no wrong way to write an ethical will. Speak from the heart.

## FAQ
**Q: Can I edit a finalized directive?**
A: Within 72 hours of finalizing, you can unseal it to make changes. After that, you'll need to create a new version.

**Q: Who sees my directives?**
A: Only you can see them while you're alive. After your passing, they are delivered to the recipients you designated.
