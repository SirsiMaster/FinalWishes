# Life Chapters — How To Guide

## What Are Life Chapters?

Life Chapters let you organize your life story into meaningful themes. Instead of scrolling through a single timeline, you can create chapters like:

- **Growing Up in Chicago** (1985 - 2003)
- **Military Service** (2003 - 2010)
- **Building Our Family** (2012 - Present)
- **Career Milestones** (2010 - Present)

Each chapter has a title, description, date range, and a collection of entries from across your estate (Soul Log entries, memories, letters, and more).

## How to Create a Chapter

1. Open **My Legacy > Life Chapters** from the sidebar
2. Click **New Chapter**
3. Enter a title, description, and optional date range
4. Click **Create Chapter**

## Adding Entries to a Chapter

1. Click any chapter card to expand it
2. Available entries from your Soul Log and Memories appear at the bottom
3. Click the **+** button next to any entry to add it to the chapter
4. Entries can belong to multiple chapters

## Editing a Chapter

Click the **pencil icon** on any chapter card to update its title, description, or date range.

## Archiving a Chapter

Click the **trash icon** on any chapter card. Archiving a chapter does NOT delete the entries within it - they remain in your estate. You're only removing the organizational grouping.

## Who Can See Life Chapters?

- **Estate Owner & Admin**: Full access (create, edit, archive)
- **Executor**: Can view chapters (read-only)
- **Heir**: Can view chapters (read-only) - this is how they discover your life story

## Tips

- Start with broad chapters and refine them over time
- Date ranges are optional - some chapters span your whole life ("Things I'm Grateful For")
- Use the chapter description to explain why this period matters to you
- Your heirs will see chapters as a curated narrative, not a data dump
